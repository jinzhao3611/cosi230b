Background

Semantic Role Labeling identifies the predicate (action) in a sentence and labels the semantic roles of its arguments (participants).

Predicate: The verb or action word that structures the meaning.

Arguments: The participants and circumstances of the action.

Common Semantic Roles

ARG0 Agent (doer of action)
ARG1 Patient/Theme (affected entity)
ARG2 Instrument/Beneficiary/Attribute
ARGM-LOC Location
ARGM-TMP Time
ARGM-MNR Manner
ARGM-CAU Cause/Reason
ARGM-PRP Purpose

Key Takeaway

SRL requires understanding the relationship between syntax and semantics, and recognizing that the same semantic role can appear in different grammatical positions.

  • Active/passive changes syntax but not semantic roles
  • Some arguments are implicit but semantically present
  • Verb-specific frames define what arguments are expected
  • Modifier vs. core argument distinction requires careful guidelines
