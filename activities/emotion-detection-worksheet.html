Background

Emotion detection identifies the emotional states expressed in text. Unlike sentiment (positive/negative), emotions are specific states like joy, anger, fear, and sadness.

Emotion vs. Sentiment:

  • Sentiment: Positive, Negative, Neutral (valence)
  • Emotion: Joy, Sadness, Anger, Fear, Surprise, Disgust (discrete categories)

Key Takeaway

Emotion annotation requires deciding whose emotions to capture, at what granularity, and acknowledging that emotional interpretation varies by person and culture.

  • Author intent ≠ Reader response ≠ Content emotion
  • Basic emotions may oversimplify nuanced states
  • Intensity adds important information beyond category
  • Cultural backgrounds affect emotional interpretation
